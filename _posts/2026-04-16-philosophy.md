Can’t really fall asleep. Two weeks ago, some emergency broke my regular sleep cycle, and till today, I still struggle to sleep at my usual hour. And each day there’s something on my mind to think about.

Today it’s about whether to do a PhD in philosophy. 

I’ve always been interested in general knowledge, that is, knowledge in the abstract way. I do not really care about particulars. Science gets me, especially physics. Maths gets me. Philosophy gets me. My love in literature is a compensation to the oversimplicity I get from abstract knowledge. Music as well. Content is also fascinating, but I am still mostly interested in the form. 

Form makes content possible. Abstract makes concrete understandable. If the world is a pile of things, without form and pattern, I would be very disappointed. 

Pattern is even more interesting when it is possible to be filled with content. Maths is interesting, logic is interesting, but the fact that the world can be put on top of them and make itself reasonable is a new level of satisfaction. 

I really enjoy it when I can spend an afternoon walking, and thinking through a very simple philosophical problem. I do not enjoy it when I am overwhelmed with historical or political details. 

I have been fighting against the idea of a degree in philosophy, because of its bad reputation in being too abstract. But I am thinking probably I can do it together with science. The philosophy of science. I am not sure, but it sounds like something I would keep being passionate for a couple of decades. That is to say, the rest of my life. 

Simplicity is really an aesthetic. Even scientists choose to believe in the simplist theories. because of its beauty. People would argue that simpler ones look closer to the truth, but what if God created everything with the most complicated way possible? It cannot be falsified. But as human beings, we love it simple. 

I am very impatient with the mess of linguistics. It adopts the principles from natural sciences. That is a bad idea. Language is not a game of chess, it is alive. It is not alive as an organism, which has a physical border. It is alive as history. It is a whole mess. It is impossible to abstract a grand theory from history. You can always be wrong. So does linguistics. 

Language is useful, but linguistics is kind of a vain effort.
